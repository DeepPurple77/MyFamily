My Family Project

Jessica Perkel, Grade 7 King David Ariel, Johannesburg, South Africa

My family project is on my Great Great Grandfather, Aron Jacob Perkel and his life starting in Adamov, Poland in 1882 and my Great Grandfather Jonah Perkel, born in 1924.

The full memoirs of Aron Jacob Perkel are available here: A J Perkel Memoirs

1898: At the age of 16, Aron Jacob started self-studying Hebrew and Russian
1900: He moved to Warsaw to apprentice as a watch maker
1904: He began working at Kalman Rootstein’s shop in Nalvechi Street in Warsaw as a watch maker
1905: Simulated a disability by paying 25 rubles to have his leg broken to avoid conscription during the Russian-Japanese War
1907: Married Toybe Sheindel in Kotzk Poland
1907 – 1923: The couple have many children during this time. Some of their children pass away due to the complications and illness. World War One occurs and life in Poland is extremely difficult, brutally cold winters and little food.
1924: The family move to Jaffa, Palestine (Israel) to start a new life.
1924: My Great Grandfather, Jonah Perkel is born in Tel Aviv. Tragically, his mother passes away days after giving birth.
1930: Aron Jacob moves to Cape Town, South Africa to try earning money to support his family. He is later joined by his second wife Feigel and 4 of his sons, Avraham, Moshe, Nathan and Jonah.
1931 to 1949: Aron Jacob moves between Cape Town, Johannesburg and Israel. Jonah (my Great Grandfather) and his brother Nathan are left in the Arcadia Orphanage in Johannesburg while his father tries to earn a living.
1950: He eventually returns to Israel to live out his remaining years.
1975: Aron Jacob passes away at the age of 93.

My Great Grandfather Jonah, lived in Arcadia from approximately 1934 to 1942. After his Barmitzvah in 1937, Jonah taught other boys in Arcadia their Barmitzvah portions and gave Chedar classes. He often lead the Shabbos services and services during Yom Tavim. Even after leaving Arcadia at the age of 18, he continued to return to teach, lead services and was an extremely highly regarded member of the community.

Jonah went on to study to be an Charted Accountant, which became his lifelong career. He married Maisie Perkel in 1948. Jonah and Maisie had 5 sons, Raymond (Raphael Mendel), Trevor (Tuvia), Colin (Nissan), Charles (Bezalel), Adrian (Arya).

Trevor Perkel, my Grandfather and my Grandmother Marilyn Friedman got married in 1975. They have 3 sons, Dale (Ephraim), Brent (Dovbear) and Craig (Chaim). Dale was born in 1977 and is my father. He married my mother, Danielle Shiller (Dina) in 2008 and they have 2 children, Sarah (Sara) and myself, Jessica (Yona). I was born in 2013 and I am currently 12 years old and will turn 13 in September 2026.

I was named after my Great Grandfather Jonah, the name Yona or Jonah means Dove. My parents named me after him as he passed away in 2010 and they wanted to give me a connection to him. Jonah was a well-respected person in the community, he was successful in business and even testified in the Rivonia Treason Trial in support of Nelson Mandela and the ANC. Jonah was 84 when he died, leaving behind his 5 sons and wife Maisie. Maisie passed away in 2017, I was fortunate enough to have met her and fondly remember her.

From about 1930 to 1969, Aron Jacob created a diary called his memoirs in which he chronicled his life. The memoirs were originally hand-written in Hebrew by Aron Jacob, they were later translated into English by my Great Grandfather Jonah and finally typed and digitized so that the memoirs would remain a valued piece of our family history.
